EasyNavigator

User study page

What does it do?

EasyNavigator aims to provide a faster way of browsing the web. It allows you to explore open tabs, lets you mark them for quicker access, and navigate between them. It also intelligently displays links to pages you tend to visit often so that repeating browsing activity is much more rapid.

Quick start

Install the extension and open one of your favorite web pages. Click on the new icon that appeared on the Chrome toolbar after installation. This shows a popup with a list of your recently and frequently visited pages within that website. Click 'Mark' and observe the ★ that appears in the tab title. Switch to another tab and mark that as well. The popup should show the two tabs in the 'Marked tabs' list. Click on a tab in the list to switch to it or use Ctrl+Shift+Z to switch between all marked tabs. This is especially useful when you have many tabs open. Open a few more pages in new tabs, click on the icon once again and select 'Explore tabs'. This displays all open tabs compactly as a 'tree' which you can interact with. Click on one of the red circles to focus on that part of the tree. Click on a tab icon to switch to it, or hover over it for a preview of the tab. ★ can be used to mark a tab. The tabs can be organized by website, category (eg. news; automatically set), or by the sequence in which you opened them.

Keyboard shortcuts

Some shortcuts may be unavailable if they conflict with other installed extensions. To fix this, open the web page chrome://extensions (copy and paste) and click on "Keyboard shortcuts" at the bottom.

  • Ctrl+Shift+X

    Show main popup with quick-links.

  • Ctrl+Shift+S

    Mark or unmark current tab.

  • Ctrl+Shift+Z

    Switch to the next marked tab.

  • Ctrl+Shift+V

    Show visualization of tabs.

Installation and permissions

EasyNavigator requires three permissions: 'Access your data on all websites' (to get the tab previews and to communicate with opendns.com), 'Read and modify your browsing history' (to show frequently/recently visited pages), and 'Access your tabs and browsing activity' (to access open tabs).

After installation, depending on the number of icons already displayed, the address bar might need to be shrunk to see the EasyNavigator icon.

EasyNavigator communicates with OpenDNS to get the categories and only sends the host name of websites (eg. for google.com/search?q=... , only google.com will be sent). EasyNavigator does not send anything else from your browsing activity to anyone, so you may rest assured that your privacy is not violated.
